INFRARED THERMAL TECHNOLOGY

infrared technology

Infrared radiation is a form of electromagnetic energy emitted by atoms and molecules in all objects with temperatures above absolute zero (-273°C). Infrared (“below” or “beyond” red) lies between the visible and microwave spectrum. When infrared waves encounter an object, they can reflect, diffract, refract, transmit, or be absorbed, often simultaneously. Invisible to humans, infrared starts at 0.76 microns, extending to 10.0 microns. All objects emit infrared energy based on their temperature.

Infrared Emitters

Infrared emitters are widely used in industrial heating applications. They provide an efficient and effective solution for various processes, from welding to curing and drying. Infrared radiation is ideal for these processes because it directly transfers heat energy to the heated object without needing air or other mediums as a transfer agent.

This makes ir emitters extremely energy-efficient compared to traditional convection heating methods, as they require no extra energy to be wasted on heating the air around them.

FLIR Thermal Cameras

Thermal cameras are a valuable tool for many industries. They use infrared technology to capture images in complete darkness and can detect minute temperature differences, making them perfect for surveillance, security, night vision, and industrial maintenance applications. Thermal cameras help operators quickly identify potential issues that may otherwise go unnoticed, allowing them to make faster decisions and more effective repairs.

Thermal cameras are becoming increasingly popular for industrial heating applications due to their numerous benefits, including:

Safety is a top priority in any industrial environment. Thermal cameras can help ensure workers are not exposed to excessive heat or dangerous radiation levels.

They provide better temperature control than traditional methods. This makes it easier to maintain optimum temperatures throughout industrial processes.

They can help save money in the long run by reducing energy costs. The ability to monitor temperatures more closely means that equipment can be adjusted as necessary to ensure it's using the least amount of energy possible.

Emitted Energy Corporation's FLIR thermal cameras are revolutionary devices allowing you to see more than ever. These advanced tools will enable you to detect and measure heat and spot subtle temperature differences that can be used to identify potential problems or diagnose issues.
